D — Denial of Service

Denial of Service is about making a service unavailable—by exhausting time, memory, bandwidth, or critical limits. Availability is the target.

What it means

Attackers overwhelm a service so legitimate users can’t access it (traffic floods, expensive requests, resource exhaustion).

Why it matters

Downtime breaks trust and costs money. Even short outages can stop payments, logins, and customer support.

How to mitigate

Rate-limit, cache, add timeouts, queue work, and scale horizontally. Use WAF/CDN and monitor thresholds before the service tips over.
